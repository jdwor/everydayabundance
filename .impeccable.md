## Design Context

### Users
General web readers arriving via social shares and newsletters, plus a thoughtful
policy/think-tank audience (the author works at Open Philanthropy). They are reading
a standalone interactive essay — "The Limit of Human Felicity / Everyday Utopia" —
about how once-miraculous technologies (recorded music, electric light, vaccines,
clean water) became invisible "furniture." The job to be done is to *feel* and
*remember* the wonder of everyday abundance, not to complete a task.

### Brand Personality
Editorial, literary, quietly authoritative — three words: **considered, warm,
wondrous**. The voice is that of a well-made magazine feature or museum wall text:
confident, unhurried, never gimmicky. Emotional goal: a calm sense of awe and
gratitude, with moments of delight as the reader moves through the illustrated room.

### Aesthetic Direction
Warm paper / print aesthetic. Cream paper palette (`--paper #f3ead7`), serif display
and body type (Iowan Old Style / Palatino), restrained accents (ink, blue `#154f65`,
red `#b74735`, gold, green), hairline rules, dotted/grid paper texture, custom
hand-illustrated room with a scroll-driven pan-and-zoom "camera."
- **Light mode only** — the paper warmth is core; no dark theme.
- **References (the right feel):** longform editorial scrollytelling (NYT/Pudding-style),
  museum exhibition typography, vintage print.
- **Anti-references (NOT this):** generic SaaS/dashboard UI, AI-slop (cyan-on-dark,
  purple gradients, glassmorphism, uniform shadowed card grids), neon, dark-mode-with-glow.

### Design Principles
1. **Space and type carry hierarchy** — favor whitespace, scale, and weight over
   boxes, borders, and color. Not everything needs a card.
2. **Rhythm over uniformity** — alternate tight grouping with generous separation;
   create deliberate emphasis moments rather than evenly-weighted repetition.
3. **Asymmetry feels authored** — left-aligned, broken-grid compositions over
   centered, evenly-distributed ones.
4. **Restraint is the brand** — every decorative element must earn its place; when in
   doubt, remove. The wonder is in the content, not the chrome.
5. **Accessible and resilient by default** — honor reduced motion, keep contrast at
   WCAG AA, real focus indicators, sensible touch targets.
